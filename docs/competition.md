# Competitive reality — student research outreach

_From the research pass, 2026-07-13. The honest version: this wedge is NOT empty._

## The wedge is already contested

At least nine tools already do some form of "find professors + AI-personalized outreach for students":

| Tool | What it does | Key gap |
|---|---|---|
| **ResearchMatch** | 250M papers; ranks profs by impact; AI summaries; finds email; "responsiveness score"; an email **quality checker** — deliberately won't auto-write | Dumps 100% of writing on the student; no scaffold; assumes credentials (no HS); ranks by impact not receptivity; no verification of claims |
| **coldmatch.ai** | Finds profs, analyzes work, **auto-generates** the email | One-click auto-write = the AI-spam professors delete; no grounding/verification; no HS handling |
| **Profeasy** | Match by "research overlap" + generate cold emails | Thin; generation-centric; no verification, no anti-spam |
| **InternLink** | Resume→matches→"personalised in 2s"→follow-up engine; planned **bulk campaigns** | Speed/scale over authenticity; no grounding; planned bulk = spray-and-pray |
| **ResearchPilot** | Grad/PhD suite: matches + SOP + scholarships + outreach generator | Grad-focused; a mile wide; email quality shallow; no verification |
| **NextMatch.ai** | PubMed-grounded outreach for medical grads (IMGs) | Wrong audience/domain; still auto-writes in <30s |
| **GPTina** | Free template generator (ChatGPT wrapper) | No discovery, no grounding, generic output |
| **FacultyFinder** | Faculty discovery/matching | Tiny DB (281 faculty); discovery only |
| **Whali** | Student cold-outreach on 275M profiles, auto-write + follow-up | Corporate networking, not academic; grounds on LinkedIn not papers; volume-oriented |

Plus adjacent: B2B cold-email tools (Smartlead, Lyne, Instantly…); pay-to-play mentor mills
(**Polygence, Lumiere, RISE, Pioneer** — $1k–$10k, they *assign* a mentor, they don't help you
earn a real position); AI admissions tools (AdmitYogi, Kollegio, Empowerly — treat research as a
resume checkbox); point utilities (Hunter.io, Google Scholar, Text Blaze).

## The market splits into two failing camps

1. **Auto-writers / spray-and-pray** (coldmatch, Profeasy, InternLink, ResearchPilot, all B2B):
   optimize speed + scale → produce exactly the AI-smelling email professors delete, and cite
   papers without verifying they exist or fit.
2. **Purists / assigners:** ResearchMatch refuses to write at all (right instinct, no scaffold);
   mentor mills sell a $1k–$10k mentor; admissions tools treat research as a checkbox.

## The empty middle = the sharpened Bruce wedge

A **grounded, human-in-the-loop, anti-spam outreach copilot for HS + early-undergrad students**,
defined by five things none of them do:

1. **Grounding WITH verification** — quote the finding from the real abstract, template-inject the
   exact title/DOI/name, entailment-check every claim before it can be sent. Credibility is the
   whole game; competitors gamble it.
2. **Human-in-the-loop by design** — grounded skeleton + a forced student-written question. Raises
   the floor (unlike ResearchMatch) without removing the human (unlike the one-click generators).
3. **Fit-gating, not impact-ranking** — rank by active + receptive + realistic-for-you; down-rank
   titans/emeriti; **tell the student not to send** when it's a bad fit.
4. **High-school credibility framing (genuinely empty niche)** — honest handling of thin
   credentials, and surface **PhD-student/postdoc first authors** as better-bandwidth targets, not
   just PIs.
5. **Anti-spam / deliverability as the core value prop** — ≤2/dept, small batches + cooldown,
   automated name-swap block, no send authority. Market *restraint*.

**Positioning:** _"Get a real, free research position by actually being interesting"_ — explicitly
against both the AI-spam generators and the $10k pay-to-play mentor mills.

**Distribution moat:** this is the only stance a professor would endorse, which unlocks a channel
the spray-and-pray tools structurally can't touch — **faculty and university career-center
partnerships.** That endorsement + the verification layer is the defensible wedge.

## Honest read

Being late to a contested wedge is survivable only by being genuinely different on a felt
dimension. The differentiation here (verification + HS framing + anti-spam + "don't send") is real
and unoccupied — but "generic AI professor outreach for students" is already tool #10 in a crowd.
Narrow hard or don't bother.
